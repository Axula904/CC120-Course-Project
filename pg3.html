Push Back

Despite the hundreds of thousands people who have come out over the years to protest for immigration reform, not too much has changed in the past decades, one could argue that the situation has even worsened due to the more and more evident political divides in the United States. However, in recent years the spreading of stories of resistance has begun to educate more and more people about the push for immigration reform.

Stories of Resistance

American Friends Service Committeeis one organization that works to spread and promote awareness through stories. Two of these stories being the experiences of Jeanette Vizguerra and Arturo Hernandez Garcia are quite similar as they give insight into what living in the United States as an undocumented individual can be like. Jeanette, a mother of 4, was pulled over due to a minor traffic violation, and after the discovery by the police that she was undocumented, she was taken into custody by ICE before seeking sanctuary. Similarly, Arturo had to spend over 9 months in sanctuary avoiding ICE. This pursuit of sanctuary is one way in which undocumented immigrants have been able to resist deportation.
Inevitably in response to stories like Jeanette and Arturos, America has begun its work to try and make seeking sanctuary harder if not impossible. One of the ways in which these bills would possibly work would be to increase the punishment for those who support sanctuaries. But, the ironic part about these proposed bills to stricken sanctuary rights is that these bills are based on the false assumption that immigrants are inherently more criminal, when in fact some studies have shown that in areas where immigration increases, crime rates decrease. Thankfully, people have taken to the streets to protest these horrendous proposed bills.
